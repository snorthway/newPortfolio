Visualizing Productivity

Over the course of a semester, I recorded the time I spent on every single (productive) out-of-class activity and visualized the results with d3.js.

It was valuable as an exercise in consistency and holding myself accountable. Later I used it as an excuse to teach myself d3. I learned a lot about my habits, and now you can scrutinize them yourself! Disclaimer: this particular demo is not responsive, so it'll only look good if you're on something 960+ pixels wide.

User-Oriented Collaborative Design: Smart Soil

My UOCD team worked with farmers who ran community supported agriculture programs (CSAs) to design a product based on their needs and values. We ended up designing the concept for a distributed sensing network for soil health metrics, which we called "Smart Soil". This included an application that would present the data in actionable terms: for example, how to plan next year's crops and when/where to amend the soil. Physarum Experiment

Minimum spanning tree of the most populous cities in the Greater Boston Area, superimposed on the slime mold network.

As an individual project for Sustainable Design class, I decided to explore biomimicry by studying how slime mold forms networks to transport nutrients. I conducted an experiment to attempt to simulate Boston's transportation systems with the emergent structures of the slime mold physarum polycephalum. In a petri dish, I laid out food sources in the arrangement of Boston's most populous cities (I put salt where the ocean would be to discourage formation of plasmodia there).

As part of the experiment I built an intervalometer for my SLR and set it up to do a time-lapse of the process. I also wrote a minimum spanning tree algorithm which I implemented in d3.js, which generated the graph on the left. Many things did not go as planned with this experiment, but I learned a lot in the process.
